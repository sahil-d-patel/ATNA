## Final Takeaways
1. The domestic airport network is robust in aggregate but structurally uneven.
2. Criticality cannot be captured by traffic alone; bridge and cross-community roles are essential.
3. Scenario outcomes show that topological position drives disruption depth and spread.
4. Vulnerability ranking offers a practical resilience-prioritization tool.

## Evidence Chain Across Visuals
- `network_map_min_analysis_weight_5.png` establishes concentrated backbone structure.
- `community_map_2025-12.png` reveals non-trivial modular organization.
- `top_5_critical_routes.png` identifies route-level structural choke points.

## Decision-Relevant Outcome
- Use ranked vulnerabilities and critical routes to target redundancy investments.
- Use scenario comparisons to stress-test contingency plans before disruptions occur.
- Use snapshot reruns over time to detect emerging structural fragility.

## Limitations To State Clearly
- Structural and snapshot-bound: not a full operational delay prediction system.
- Results are strongest for relative ranking/triage within each monthly run.
- Broader policy claims should be paired with operational and economic context.

## Recommended Closing Statement
"This analysis turns complex national air network behavior into interpretable structural risk signals: where the network is concentrated, where it is fragile, and where intervention has the highest resilience payoff."
